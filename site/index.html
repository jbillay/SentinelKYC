The pipeline, unbundled

Six agents. One case file.

Each stage of due diligence is an independent agent — enable, disable, and tune any of them at runtime. Disable one and the system degrades honestly: a partially assessed case always lands with a human reviewer.

01 / entity-resolution

Entity resolution

Searches the registry, scores candidates deterministically, and pauses for analyst confirmation. Built for vendor composition — Companies House as the free base, premium data vendors layered on top with per-field attribution.

02 / document-manager

Document intelligence

Pulls the latest filings, extracts the text layer, and OCRs only the pages worth reading. Shareholders, financials, and subscribers come out as structured data — with provenance and confidence on every record.

03 / ubo-structure

UBO & ownership structure

Resolves every officer, PSC, and shareholder into a cross-dossier party master — the same person on three dossiers is one screened identity — and renders the ownership graph for review.

04 / screening

Sanctions & adverse media

Fuzzy name matching against locally cached OFAC SDN and UK HMT lists, plus live news screening. An LLM judges each potential hit — and biases toward human review when identifiers are thin.

05 / risk-assessment

Deterministic risk

A weighted-factor engine — geography, entity type, structural complexity, industry — under a versioned risk matrix your compliance team edits in the UI. Screening knockouts can floor or force the tier; the LLM writes the rationale, never the score.

06 / qa

QA gate & routing

A pure completeness-and-consistency engine routes every case: low risk auto-approves, anything else goes to streamlined or standard review — with a regulator-style narrative memo written for the file.

Built for the audit

If a regulator asks “why?”, the answer is already filed.

Automation you can't explain is risk you can't accept. SentinelKYC treats the audit trail as the product, not a by-product.

Every decision is a record

Each pipeline step writes a decision fragment — inputs, outputs, timing, and outcome — nested down to the individual screening hit.

Humans outrank models

Reviewers override any LLM judgement on the record, with a reason. Overrides carry forward across runs and across dossiers at the party level.

Runs are diffable

Re-run a company a month later and diff the two dossiers — what changed, what was screened, how the risk moved and why.

Deterministic where it matters

Entity scoring, screening risk rules, the risk matrix, and QA routing are pure engines. Same inputs, same outputs, every time — LLMs draft and judge, they never decide alone.

Configuration is versioned

Prompts, the risk matrix, and every agent's settings are append-only versions with an immutable change log — who changed what, and when.
